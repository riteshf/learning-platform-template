### 1. What Are Core Concepts?

Core concepts form the foundation upon which all advanced topics build. Think of them as the alphabet before writing poetry.

**Key Points:**

- Concepts are abstract ideas that organize information
- Each concept has properties, relationships, and applications
- Understanding relationships between concepts is as important as understanding individual concepts

**Real-World Analogy:**
Just as you need to understand "cells," "organisms," and "ecosystems" in biology, our field has its own hierarchy of concepts that build upon each other.

### 2. Historical Context

Understanding where these concepts came from helps us understand why they matter.

**Timeline:**

- **Phase 1**: Early foundations (1980s-1990s)
  - Pioneer researchers established basic principles
  - First implementations and use cases emerged
- **Phase 2**: Standardization (2000s)
  - Industry convergence around best practices
  - Formal specifications and standards published
- **Phase 3**: Modern era (2010s-present)
  - Tools and frameworks simplified adoption
  - Cloud and distributed systems accelerated evolution
